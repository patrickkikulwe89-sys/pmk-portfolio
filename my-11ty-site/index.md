<section>
    <h2>Personal Bio</h2>
    
    <h3>Origins & Identity</h3>
    <p>I was born in 1989 at Nsambya Hospital to Mr. and Mrs. John Baptist Ssuuna Walugembe of Kibuye, Kampala, Uganda. I lived briefly with my paternal grandmother until age 3, and then grew up at my paternal grandfather's home until I married my wonderful wife, Mrs. Kikulwe, on June 30, 2021. Over the years, I've gathered a few nicknames: <strong>Rickie</strong> (from my youth), <strong>Ssuuna Jnr.</strong> (which I derived from my father's last name), and <strong>Ka-engine</strong>, given to me by colleagues at Fang Fang Restaurant due to my swift, tireless, and hardworking nature. I have always loved to work, regardless of the industry.</p>
    
    <h3>The Spark of Technology</h3>
    <p>My love for technology started early. Around the age of 10, people in my community would bring me their faulty cellphones, and to my own surprise, I was actually able to fix them. I didn't even know what an "IT course" meant back then, but I knew I wanted to do it. During my O-levels, friends and I would frequent an internet cafe in Bakuli to surf the web. Friends like Steven Maina (a PLE O.B.) and Jeff helped sharpen my skills in my early twenties. And a special blessing goes to my "Muvabulaaya" grandpa who helped me acquire my very first official credential in tech.</p>

    <h3>Resilience & Education</h3>
    <p>My journey into the professional world started early due to the loss of my beloved grandfather, who was my source of school funding and meant everything to me. I began working at 17, hoping to gather enough finances to bring myself back to school. Academically, when I finally started the IT journey I had dreamt of, I struggled to get the guidance I needed from teachers. Because I am someone who refuses to just coast through a course without truly understanding it, I made the bold choice to switch my major from Applied Tech to Applied Business—a decision I don't regret one bit.</p>

    <h3>Purpose & Values</h3>
    <p>My being in software today has God written all over it. I am a problem solver at heart, especially for people. My wife gave me the nickname <strong>"Volu"</strong> (derived from Volunteering) because I happily offer my tech services for free, driven by love and passion. I firmly believe that if God created me for a specific mission, it is to offer support to others. Rooted in my beliefs as a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, I value integrity above all else. I have walked away from work and money when it compromised my standards, and I have absolutely no regrets. I am exactly where I am meant to be.</p>
